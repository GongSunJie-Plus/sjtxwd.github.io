  Around 1500, Middle English evolved into Modern English. The spelling letters used in modern English are also completely borrowed from 26 letters. The so-called "English alphabet" is the spelling letter used by ancient Romans in writing. English began to use the Latin alphabet as a spelling system around the sixth century A.D. in Anglo-Saxon times. The missionaries introduced letters in order to record the local language into words. The problem they faced was that there were more than 40 different pronunciations in English at that time, but the Latin letters could not correspond to each other. So they used the methods of adding letters, adding mutants to letters, and connecting two letters to correspond to different pronunciations. Later, a system of 26 Latin letters plus & with some spelling rules was formed in Old English.

IT

  Computer science (sometimes abbreviated as CS) is the theoretical basis of systematic research on information and computing and the practical technology of how they are implemented and applied in computer systems. It is often described as a systematic study of algorithmic processing for creating, describing and transforming information. Computer science embraces many branches; some emphasize the calculation of specific results, such as computer graphics; some explore the nature of computing problems, such as computational complexity theory; and some focus on how to implement computing, such as programming language theory is the method of describing computing, while programming It is the application of specific programming languages to solve specific computing problems, while human-computer interaction focuses on how to make computers and computing useful, easy to use, and anytime, anywhere for people to use. [1]
  Sometimes the public mistakenly think that computer science is the cause of solving computer problems (such as information technology), or that it is only related to the experience of using computers, such as playing games, surfing the Internet or word processing. In fact, computer science focuses not only on understanding the nature of programs that implement software like games and browsers, but also on creating new programs or improving existing ones through existing knowledge.
  Computer is a kind of machine for arithmetic and logic operation, and there are communication problems for systems connected by several computers, and the object of processing is information. Therefore, computer science is the science of information processing. Computer science consists of theoretical computer science and experimental computer science. Computer Science in mathematical literature generally refers to theoretical computer science. Experimental computer science also includes research on opening up new areas of computer applications. [1]
  Most of the research in computer science is based on Von Neumann computers and Turing machines, which are the computational models of most real machines. As the founder of this model, Church-Turing Thesis shows that although there may be differences in computing time and space efficiency, existing computing devices have the same computing power. Although this theory is generally considered the basis of computer science, scientists also study other kinds of machines, such as parallel computers at the practical level and probabilistic computers, Oracle computers and quantum computers at the theoretical level. In this sense, computers are just a computing tool: a famous computer scientist, Dijkstra, has a famous saying: "Computer science pays less attention to computers than astronomy to telescopes."
  As a discipline, computer science covers from the theoretical research and computational limits of algorithms to how to implement computational systems through hardware and software. CSAB (formerly known as Computing Sciences Accreditation Board) is composed of representatives of Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) and IEEE Computer Society (IEEE-CS). It establishes four main fields of computer science: computing theory, algorithms and data structures, programming methods and programming languages, and design. Computer Elements and Architecture. CSAB also establishes other important areas, such as software engineering, artificial intelligence, computer networks and communications, database systems, parallel computing, distributed computing, human-computer interaction, machine translation, computer graphics, operating systems, and numerical and symbolic computing.
